# Competitor Research: Klai Scribe

**Product context:** Klai Scribe is a private EU-hosted transcription product with three input methods: (1) browser recording (Record tab), (2) file upload, (3) meeting bot. Audio is never stored. For upload/record flows, transcripts are only saved when the user explicitly chooses. For meeting bot sessions, the transcript is automatically saved after the meeting ends (user gave consent before inviting the bot) and can be deleted at any time. Data stays in the EU.

**Competitors researched:** Fireflies.ai and Otter.ai
**Research date:** 2026-03-24
**Pages scraped:** fireflies.ai, fireflies.ai/features, fireflies.ai/security, fireflies.ai/pricing, otter.ai, otter.ai/features, otter.ai/pricing, otter.ai/privacy-policy

## Comparative analysis for Klai Scribe

### What both competitors lead with

Both Fireflies and Otter open with the AI notetaker frame ("The #1 AI Notetaker", "Turn meetings into transcripts"). The functional pitch is almost identical: bot joins, transcribes, summarises, integrates with CRM and project tools. Neither leads with privacy or data residency -- these are positioned late in the page (security section near the footer) and framed as enterprise compliance boxes to tick, not as core product values.

### Where Klai Scribe has a structural advantage

**EU data residency is a real differentiator, not a feature claim:**
Otter stores data on AWS US-West. Their privacy policy names SCCs and the EU-US DPF as the legal basis for cross-border transfer -- not actual EU storage. For EU organisations in regulated sectors (healthcare, legal, finance, government), this is a genuine compliance risk that Klai can name directly.

Fireflies does not publish server locations. "Private Storage" is Enterprise-only and positioned as a premium add-on. Default storage location is opaque.

**Klai's audio-never-stored is a genuine position, not a tier:**
Both competitors offer zero-data-training promises (Fireflies: "0-day retention for AI training"), but data is still stored and searchable. Fireflies charges for unlimited storage at Business tier. Otter limits storage minutes on lower tiers to drive upgrades. Klai's model: audio is never retained in any flow; transcripts are in your account and deletable any time. That's structurally different from competitors where storage accumulates by design.

**Privacy as product architecture, not compliance language:**
Both competitors say "GDPR compliant" but rely on SCCs and DPF frameworks, meaning EU user data leaves the EU. Klai can say the data never left the EU, full stop. That is a factual architectural claim, not a legal framework claim.

### Messaging patterns worth adopting or countering

**Worth adapting for Klai:**
- The "bot joins your meeting" explanation is clear and practical; Fireflies uses the email invite framing ("invite fireflies@fireflies.ai") which is concrete and reassuring
- Both use strong quantified social proof early ("4+ hours saved", "33% time back") -- Klai will need equivalent claims
- "Like having your own executive assistant" (Otter) is effective aspirational framing
- The action items and CRM sync use case has strong pull for sales and customer success teams

**Fireflies privacy language to study:**
- "Zero Data Retention -- your data is never used for AI training" is a strong claim but actually refers to AI training only, not to storage itself
- "Customer Own Their Data" sounds strong but doesn't address where it's stored or who can access it under US law (CLOUD Act)

**The CLOUD Act angle:**
Neither competitor mentions the CLOUD Act. For EU enterprise buyers, the fact that Otter and Fireflies are US companies storing data on US infrastructure means that data is technically accessible to US federal agencies under the CLOUD Act, regardless of GDPR SCCs. This is a legal risk that Klai -- as a EU-hosted, EU-operated product -- does not carry. This angle is most relevant for regulated-sector sales conversations.

### Positioning gap Klai should own

Neither competitor positions privacy as a core product value or architectural choice. Both treat it as a compliance checkbox near the bottom of the page. Klai Scribe can own the frame: **private by design, not by policy**. The data stays in the EU not because of a legal agreement, but because it never leaves.

## Klai Scribe: what's actually built (code audit 2026-03-24)

**Meeting bot:** Vexa integration in `klai-portal/backend/app/services/vexa.py`. Methods: `start_bot`, `stop_bot`, `get_recording`. Supports Google Meet, Zoom, Teams. Users add an email address to their calendar invite. This is the correct "invite Klai" framing.

**File upload:** `POST /v1/transcribe` in `klai-scribe/scribe-api/app/api/transcribe.py` processes audio/video files without saving. A separate `POST /v1/transcriptions` endpoint saves explicitly.

**Speaker attribution:** `transcript_segments` JSONB field in `klai-portal/backend/app/models/meetings.py`. Speaker-attributed transcripts are a real, implemented feature.

**Retention model:** Three flows with different save behavior:
- Upload/Record: creates a `TranscriptionDraft` — NOT auto-saved. User explicitly POSTs to `/v1/transcriptions` to persist.
- Meeting bot (Vexa): transcript is ALWAYS auto-saved via webhook after meeting ends. User gave consent before bot joined (`consent_given: true`). User can delete afterward.
- Audio: NEVER stored in any flow. Whisper processes and discards it.

**Transcription engine:** Self-hosted Whisper (HTTP provider). Do NOT use "Whisper" in user-facing copy -- it means nothing to users. Say "processed on EU servers" or "open source transcription model."

**Knowledge integration:** Transcripts can be saved to Qdrant `klai_knowledge` collection via `klai-portal/backend/app/api/knowledge.py`.

**Copy framework (validated):**
- Lead with: "Never take meeting notes again." (the moment, not the privacy)
- Then: three ways to capture (meeting bot joins / upload a recording / record in browser)
- Then: what you get: full transcript, speaker attribution
- Then: retention truth — audio never stored; transcripts in your account, deletable any time; meeting bot always saves after the meeting (with prior consent)
- Privacy as unlock layer: "and all of this on EU infrastructure, not US servers"
- NOT: GDPR Article references, specific server locations invented, "Whisper" as a brand name
- NOT: summaries or action items (not yet implemented in the API)
