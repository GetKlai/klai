## Perplexity AI

Perplexity's homepage is a search interface (no landing page copy). The following is drawn from their help center.

### Positioning (from "What is Perplexity?")

> "Perplexity is an AI-powered search engine that transforms how you discover and interact with information. Simply ask any question, and it searches the web to deliver accessible, conversational answers backed by verifiable sources. Each response includes citations and links to original sources, enabling you to verify the information and explore topics in greater depth."

### What sets Perplexity apart (their own framing)

> "Traditional search engines present you with lots of links to sift through. Perplexity functions as an intelligent research assistant, streamlining your information gathering by delivering the precise knowledge you need without the extra steps and clicks."

Four differentiators they name explicitly:

1. **Direct answers** — "Receive comprehensive responses that synthesize information from multiple sources, eliminating the need to navigate through numerous web pages to find what you're looking for."
2. **Current information** — "Content is sourced from the web in real-time as you ask your questions, ensuring you receive the most up-to-date information available."
3. **Credible sources** — "All responses are supported by citations from reputable news organizations, academic publications, and established content sources."
4. **Adaptive search experience** — "Whether you're conducting in-depth research for professional work, seeking quick answers to everyday questions, or exploring new subjects, Perplexity offers both standard and advanced search features alongside AI assistance to help you find information more efficiently than conventional search engines."

### Homepage prompt categories (what they suggest users do)

- Build a business
- Create a prototype
- Organize my life
- Help me learn
- Monitor the situation

### Notable: Perplexity is web-first, not document-first

Perplexity searches the open web in real time. Document upload ("Files") exists as a feature but is not their primary positioning. Their differentiator vs. Google is answer synthesis + citations, not private document grounding.

Relevance to Klai Focus: Perplexity occupies the "AI search" category; NotebookLM occupies the "private document AI" category. Klai Focus competes primarily with NotebookLM, not Perplexity.

## Observations and opportunities for Klai Focus

### What NotebookLM does well (to learn from)

- **"Understand Anything"** is a strong, open-ended, ambition-level headline — not feature-focused
- The three-step explanation (upload → expert → cite) is clean and instantly legible
- **"See the source, not just the answer"** is a great feature headline; it names the core trust mechanism
- **"Personalized AI expert in the information that matters most to you"** is the core value prop in one sentence
- The use case structure (what you upload + what NotebookLM does + outcome tagline) is a reusable content pattern
- Privacy section appears on every page — it's a persistent concern for their audience

### Where NotebookLM is weak (Klai Focus differentiation opportunities)

- **US infrastructure, Google account required** — no EU data residency, no GDPR-native guarantees
- **No self-hosting** — organizations with strict data requirements cannot use it
- **"Unless you share feedback"** is a hedge — individual data may be used for training
- **No mention of open source** — black-box model, no auditability
- **Source limit as a pricing gate** (50 / 100 / 300 / 600) — competitive pressure point
- **Privacy section is reassuring but vague** — no DPA, no EU standard contractual clauses mentioned publicly

### Copy patterns worth adapting for Klai Focus

| NotebookLM pattern | Klai Focus adaptation |
|---|---|
| "Grounded in the information you trust" | "Grounded in your documents — nothing else" |
| "See the source, not just the answer" | Reusable as-is (it's a generic truth about citation-based AI) |
| "Personalized AI expert in the information that matters most to you" | "Your documents become a private AI expert — accessible only to you" |
| "We value your privacy and never use your organization's data to train" | Klai can go harder: EU-hosted, open source, no training ever, not even for individuals |
| Use case formula: what you upload + what it does + outcome tagline | Adopt directly for Klai Focus use case section |
| "Understand Anything" (hero) | Can do better with privacy angle: "Ask anything. Your data stays yours." or "All the answers. None of the exposure." |

### Privacy angle: how far Klai can go vs. NotebookLM

NotebookLM's claim: "never use your organization's data to train" (orgs only; individuals hedged)

Klai Focus can claim:
- Hosted in the EU (GDPR-native, data never leaves EU jurisdiction)
- No training on your data — ever, for any account type, no exceptions
- Open source stack (auditable, no black box)
- Self-hostable for maximum-security requirements
- No Google account required — no dependency on US Big Tech infrastructure

This is a materially stronger privacy story, not just a slight differentiation.

## Klai Focus: what's actually built (code audit 2026-03-24)

**Source retrieval:** `klai-focus/research-api/app/services/retrieval.py`. Three research modes:
- NARROW: searches only uploaded documents (like NotebookLM's core behaviour)
- BROAD: searches uploaded documents + general LLM knowledge
- WEB: documents + SearXNG web search (your docs as context, web as supplement)

**Source types supported** (`klai-focus/research-api/app/api/sources.py`): PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX, TXT, MD, URL (web pages), YouTube videos (explicit `type: "youtube"`)

**Citations:** Answers include `[source_name, p.{page}]` references. This is a real, implemented feature. "Every answer shows exactly where it came from" is factually correct.

**Session history:** History is stored per notebook and can be turned on. "Nothing retained after your session" was incorrect -- history is configurable. Correct framing: "documents processed on EU servers; nothing shared with any third party."

**No third-party dependency:** Not processed by Google (unlike NotebookLM), no Google account required.

**Copy framework (validated):**
- Lead with: "The confidential documents you couldn't put in ChatGPT. Now you can."
- Then: upload any file type (PDF, Word, Excel, YouTube) + ask in plain language
- Then: cited answers with source reference (page number, paragraph)
- Then: three modes (just your docs / your docs + broader knowledge / docs + web search)
- Privacy as unlock: "processes on EU infrastructure; no Google account; not sent to any US service"
- NOT: "nothing is retained after your session" (history exists) -- say "processed privately on EU servers"
